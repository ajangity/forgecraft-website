Background

From Robotics Labs to Building the Future of Products

I'm studying robotics engineering at UC Santa Cruz — a program that forces you to live at the intersection of hardware and software, to build systems that actually work in the physical world, and to solve problems where "almost correct" isn't good enough.

That mindset shapes everything I build. I care about systems that are verifiably correct, not just plausible. I care about the full pipeline — from the problem statement to the delivered product. And I care about building things that actually help people, not just impressive demos.

Robotics taught me that the hardest problems aren't in any single component. They're in the integration — making all the parts work together reliably, in the real world, under conditions you didn't fully anticipate. That's exactly the problem ForgeCraft solves.

BananaBots

Shipping Real Products for Real Users

BananaBots is the engineering organization I co-founded and co-lead at UC Santa Cruz. Our mission is to build assistive technology — real products that improve quality of life for people with disabilities, not research prototypes that sit in a drawer.

Running BananaBots taught me more about building products under constraints than any class could. Limited budget, limited time, diverse team, real users with real needs — that's the environment where I learned to ship.

It also taught me how frustrating it is when the tools don't fit the problem. We constantly needed custom software for specific BananaBots workflows — data dashboards, tracking systems, onboarding tools — and building each one from scratch consumed time we needed for the actual product work. That frustration is what ForgeCraft is designed to eliminate.

Why ForgeCraft

Why I'm Building This

I've experienced the problem firsthand — trying to build the right tool for the right problem, with limited time and limited resources. ForgeCraft is the platform I wish had existed.

"Every time I needed a custom tool at BananaBots — a tracking dashboard, an onboarding system, a data pipeline — I had two choices: spend days building it from scratch, or settle for a generic tool that sort of fit. Neither was acceptable.

I kept thinking: the code for these tools is not novel. The patterns are well-understood. The only reason it takes days is because humans have to write it. AI can write it. And if AI can write it, it can also test it, verify it meets requirements, and deliver it. That's the entire ForgeCraft pipeline — built from that frustration."
— Arya Jangity, Founder, ForgeCraft
Mission & Vision

What We're Building Toward

ForgeCraft exists to remove the barrier between what people need and what they can have.

Our Mission

"Make custom product development accessible to every person and business on earth — regardless of technical skill or budget."

Today, getting a custom software product built requires either significant money ($50K+), technical expertise, or months of time. ForgeCraft removes all three barriers. Anyone with a problem and an internet connection can get a custom solution.

Our Vision

"A world where any person can commission any product — digital or physical — and receive it, built perfectly, within hours."

Software is Phase 1. Physical hardware is Phase 2. The same autonomous pipeline — describe, design, build, verify, test, deliver — works for both. ForgeCraft is the infrastructure layer for a world of infinite, affordable customization.

The era of mass production — one product for millions of people — is ending.
The era of mass customization — one perfect product for each person — is beginning.
